To address these questions, I study neutral hydrogen using radio interferometers such as the Very Large Array.

COSMOS HI Large Extragalactic Survey (CHILES)

I work with the COSMOS HI Large Extragalactic Survey (CHILES) survey, a very deep set of observations made with the VLA that allow us to trace hydrogen gas in galaxies nearly halfway back to the age of the universe (z ≈ 0.5). My research uses these data to study how the gas content of galaxies changes with redshift and environment, providing insight into how galaxies evolve within the cosmic web — the vast network of filaments and clusters that make up the large-scale structure of the universe. I have contributed to building the data processing pipelines for CHILES and to the companion Continuum & Polarization project, which has produced the most sensitive 1.4 GHz radio images of this part of the sky at sub-5'' resolution, as well as been cross-matched with the extensive multi-wavelength COSMOS catalogue.

Tiny Titans - HI

I study dwarf–dwarf galaxy interactions through the TiNy Titans HI survey, which explores how the smallest galaxies affect one another’s evolution. As part of this project, we have collected more than 300 hours of VLA observations for both neutral hydrogen and radio continuum, targeting over a dozen interacting systems. Beyond characterizing the dynamics of galaxy pairs, our work has revealed new insights into the behavior of low-mass galaxy groups and uncovered a candidate case of ram-pressure stripping driven by the cosmic web.

Other Projects (Past and Present)

In addition to CHILES and TiNy Titans, I have contributed to several other projects exploring galaxy evolution and star formation. With the GASP survey, I studied the differences between “jellyfish” galaxies undergoing ram-pressure stripping and other cluster galaxies that retain their HI gas. I have also reduced ALMA Band 3 and Band 6 observations of the Sun to investigate the physical properties of the chromosphere. Looking ahead, I plan to examine the role of active galactic nuclei (AGN) in shaping the HI distribution in merging galaxies with a VLA project that was recently rewarded 90 hours.
